🌊 The Floridan Aquifer — Your Water Source

FL's water comes from one of the world's most productive aquifers, but naturally high in hardness minerals (calcium, magnesium), sulfur compounds, and iron. FL homeowners spend more per capita on in-home water treatment than any other Southern state.

🦨 Sulfur Odor — Broward & Pasco Are Worst

H2S levels of 0.1–0.5 mg/L are common in these aquifer-fed systems. The "rotten egg" smell is strongest in the morning when water has sat in pipes overnight. Carbon filters don't remove H2S — an oxidizing filter is required.

💎 Hard Water by FL Region

SW FL (Naples) 300–400 mg/L — hardest in state. South FL (Miami-Dade/Broward) 200–280 mg/L. Tampa Bay 150–220 mg/L. Jacksonville/NE FL 80–120 mg/L (softer). Hard water shortens water heater lifespan from 12 to 7–8 years.

⚠️ PFAS Contamination in FL

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances ("forever chemicals") detected near FL military bases (Pensacola, Tyndall, Jacksonville NAS). EPA's 2024 MCL of 4 ppt for PFOA/PFOS requires treatment in affected areas. Reverse osmosis removes >90% of PFAS.

📄 FL DEP MCL Standards & Your CCR

FL adopts EPA Maximum Contaminant Levels. Your utility must provide an annual Consumer Confidence Report (CCR). Review your CCR before selecting a filtration system — target the specific contaminants present in your water.

🏚️ Well Water Warning

FL has 1.2 million private wells not regulated by FL DEP. Well water should be tested annually for coliform bacteria, nitrates, pH, hardness, iron, and H2S. A UV system is strongly recommended for any FL well water supply.

🔥 Water Heater Efficiency

Hard water scale reduces water heater element efficiency by up to 29% per DOE studies. A water softener before your water heater can add 3–5 years to its lifespan and save $100–200/year in energy costs.

📋 FL Licensing Requirement

Whole-house water treatment installation requires a FL-licensed plumber (FS 489). The plumber must pull a permit for any work involving the main water supply line or water heater connections. Verify license at myfloridalicense.com.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does water filtration cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida water filtration estimates run about $3,000-$10,000, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Timelines depend on scope - many routine jobs take a few hours to a day, while larger projects run longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm after assessing your home.
