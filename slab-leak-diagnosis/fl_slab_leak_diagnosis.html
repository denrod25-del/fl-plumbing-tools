🩺 Slab Leak Symptom Checker
Check all symptoms you're experiencing. Most slab leaks in FL start small — catching them early saves $3,000–$15,000 in foundation repair costs. 79% of FL slab leak calls involve copper pipe installed pre-1985.
✓
Water bill increased 20%+ with no change in usage
Unexplained increase on utility statement is #1 indicator. A 1/8" pin-hole in copper loses 7,000–10,000 gallons/month.
HIGH indicator
Warm or hot spot on the floor (tile or slab)
A hot water line leaking under the slab creates a distinct warm spot even through tile. Walk barefoot on cool mornings.
HIGH indicator — hot line
Sound of running water when no fixtures are on
Turn off all fixtures and appliances. If you can hear running water or hissing near the floor, likely an active slab leak.
HIGH indicator
Water meter moving with all fixtures off
Locate your water meter. Turn off everything. Watch for the low-flow indicator (triangle) spinning. Any movement = active leak.
DEFINITIVE — call now
Wet, damp, or musty spots on floor / baseboards
Water migrates up through slab or appears at base of walls. Often first spotted on carpet or near exterior doors/walls.
HIGH indicator
Tile cracking or grout separating at floor level
Soil erosion from water under the slab causes settling. Tile cracks radiate from the leak point over time. Common in South FL limestone substrate.
MEDIUM indicator
Low water pressure at one or more fixtures
A leaking supply line loses pressure to downstream fixtures. Hot water pressure dropping more than cold suggests hot-side leak.
MEDIUM indicator
Mold or mildew odor at floor level or behind walls
Chronic moisture under slab creates mold. In FL humidity, mold establishes within 24–48 hours of water intrusion. Health risk.
MEDIUM indicator
Foundation cracks appearing on interior or exterior walls
Long-term slab leaks erode the substrate under the foundation, causing differential settling and structural cracks. Urgent.
URGENT — structural risk
Home built 1955–1990 with original copper piping
High-risk era for Type M copper under slab in FL. Acidic soil pH (4.5–6.5 typical in South FL) corrodes copper from outside. Pin-holes develop at flux residue points and soil contact.
RISK FACTOR
Water heater running constantly / high gas/electric bill
Hot water slab leaks cause the water heater to run non-stop. Electric heaters especially — check if heating element cycles frequently.
MEDIUM indicator
Green staining at copper pipe fittings or shutoffs
Verdigris (copper oxidation) at visible fittings suggests pipe-wide corrosion. If visible pipes show pitting, under-slab sections likely worse.
SUPPORTING factor
🔬 Slab Leak Detection Methods
Detection before repair is essential. Pinpointing the leak location before breaking concrete saves $500–$2,000 in unnecessary excavation. FL licensed plumbers use 3 primary methods — often combined.
🎧 Acoustic Leak Detection (Electronic Listening)$300–$600
Specialized electronic amplifiers and ground microphones detect the sound of water escaping through a pipe under pressure. The technician walks a grid pattern over the slab, listening for sound amplification at the leak point.
Accuracy70–85% (varies by pipe depth, soil type, background noise)
Best forPressurized hot or cold supply leaks
LimitationsCannot locate leaks in low-pressure or drain lines
EquipmentGround microphone, pipe amplifier, correlator
FL conditionsWorks well in clay/limestone substrate; less accurate in sand fill
Typical FL cost$300–$600 (detection only, not repair)
Best first step — fast, non-invasive, no damage to slab. Most FL plumbers start here.
🌡️ Thermal Imaging (Infrared Camera)$400–$800
An infrared camera detects temperature differentials on the slab surface. Hot water leaks create distinct warm spots visible on thermal imagery. Cold water leaks show as cool zones, especially on warm FL mornings.
Accuracy80–92% for hot water leaks; 50–70% for cold
Best forHot water supply leaks — most definitive thermal signature
LimitationsTile thickness affects accuracy; cold lines harder to spot
FL timingBest scanned early morning before ambient heat builds
EquipmentFLIR or Fluke thermal camera (pro grade >320×240 resolution)
Typical FL cost$400–$800 (often combined with acoustic)
FL advantage: Thermal detection is especially effective in FL homes where tile floors are common (excellent thermal conduction vs carpet). Best in winter months.
💨 Tracer Gas Detection (Hydrogen/Nitrogen)$500–$900
A non-toxic mixture of 95% nitrogen and 5% hydrogen (or helium in some systems) is injected into the water line. The gas migrates through the leak point and up through the slab. An electronic gas sniffer precisely locates where gas is surfacing.
Accuracy95%+ — highest precision of any detection method
Best forPinpoint accuracy when acoustic gives area estimate only
ProcessShut water, inject gas, sweep slab with gas sniffer probe
Safety95%N₂/5%H₂ is non-flammable and non-toxic
Cost$500–$900 (equipment-intensive; worth it for complex leaks)
Most accurate method. Minimizes unnecessary slab breaking. Recommended when acoustic pinpoint accuracy is ±3 ft or more.
📷 Video Pipe Inspection (CCTV Camera)$250–$500
A small waterproof camera on a flexible cable is inserted into the pipe to visually inspect interior surfaces. Reveals pitting, corrosion, scale buildup, cracks, or collapsed sections. Used for drain lines that cannot be pressurized for acoustic detection.
Best forDrain line leaks, cast iron sewer inspection, pre-purchase inspection
Cannot doSupply line leaks (too small diameter; line must be clear)
OutputRecorded video with timestamp and pipe depth locator
FL use caseCast iron drain inspection in pre-1975 homes
💧 Hydrostatic Pressure Test$200–$400
The drain system is plugged at the main clean-out and filled with water to test-chamber height. Pressure is monitored — a drop confirms a drain leak. Isolating sections identifies which branch is leaking. Often used to confirm a drain leak before camera inspection.
ConfirmsWhether drain system leaks (not supply)
IsolatesWhich branch — bathroom, kitchen, main sewer
Required byMany FL insurance companies before claim approval
FL insuranceCitizens, Universal, FedNat require hydrostatic test for drain leak claims
Insurance requirement: Many FL homeowners insurance policies require a hydrostatic test before approving slab leak claims. Ask your adjuster before scheduling repair.

🔧 Slab Leak Repair Options
Three main repair strategies in FL. The right choice depends on: leak location, pipe age, number of existing leaks, insurance coverage, and long-term plans. Our licensed plumber will recommend based on your specific situation.
🏆 Reroute / Repipe (Overhead or Through Walls)
$3,500 – $8,500 (whole-house reroute)
Best long-term value No slab breaking New pipe warranty
How it works: Existing under-slab copper is abandoned in place. New PEX or CPVC supply lines are run overhead through the attic and down through walls to each fixture. No concrete is broken.

Why FL plumbers recommend this most: If your home is 1955–1990 with original copper under slab, one leak usually means the entire pipe system is in similar condition. Spot repair today means another slab leak in 2–5 years.

Materials used: PEX-A (Uponor/Rehau) or CPVC per FPC requirements. PEX is flexible, freeze-tolerant, and immune to the soil chemistry that corrodes copper.

Cost factors: Number of fixtures, home SF, attic accessibility, drywall patching, restoring wall finish.
2-bed/1-bath home (slab)$3,500–$5,500
3-bed/2-bath home$5,000–$7,500
4-bed/3-bath home$7,000–$10,500
Drywall restoration+$500–$2,500 additional
PEX pipe warranty25 years (manufacturer)
🔨 Spot Repair (Jackhammer / Open Slab)
$1,800 – $4,500 (one leak location)
Lowest upfront cost Only fixes current leak
How it works: Concrete is jackhammered at the confirmed leak location (typically a 2'×2' to 4'×4' area). The leaking pipe section is cut out, repaired with a copper coupler, and the slab is patched with new concrete.

When to consider: Newer home (post-1990), first leak in the system, isolated damage, or if reroute is not feasible due to home construction type.

FL insurance: Homeowners insurance under FL Statute §627.706 (Covered Perils — sudden accidental discharge) may cover the access cost (jackhammer, slab opening, closing) but typically NOT the pipe repair itself or finished floor restoration.

Important caveat: If the system has a history of leaks or pipe is original 1955–1985 copper, spot repair is a temporary solution.
Jackhammer & excavation$500–$1,200
Pipe repair (copper splice)$350–$800
Concrete patch & cure$400–$900
Tile restoration (over patch)$400–$1,200+
FL insurance may coverAccess/opening costs only
🔵 Epoxy Pipe Lining (CIPP)
$2,500 – $7,000 (single-pipe run)
No slab breaking Seals pitting & minor leaks Not for heavily corroded pipe
How it works: Cured-in-place pipe (CIPP) epoxy lining is blown or pulled through the existing copper pipe. The epoxy cures against the interior wall, creating a new smooth pipe within the old one. Does not require slab excavation.

Best for: Widespread pin-holing where the pipe structure is still largely intact. Seals current leaks and prevents future ones in the lined section.

Limitations: Cannot reline severely corroded or collapsed pipe. Reduces interior pipe diameter by ~1/8". Not all FL inspectors accept as final repair without spot repair verification. Ask about permit requirements in your county.

Manufacturers used in FL: Nu-Flow, Perma-Pipe, Pipe Shield.
Single hot or cold line (avg home)$2,500–$4,500
Full hot & cold system lining$5,500–$10,000
Permit required in most FL countiesYes — verify with AHJ
FL insurance coverageVaries; some carriers exclude lining
