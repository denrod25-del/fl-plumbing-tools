FL-Specific Maintenance Tips

💧 Hard Water Descaling
Florida average water hardness is 150–350 mg/L (very hard). Annual descaling of water heaters and faucet aerators prevents efficiency loss and premature element failure. Sediment buildup can cut water heater efficiency by up to 25%. Consider a whole-home water softener if your hardness exceeds 180 mg/L.
🌳 Tree Root Intrusion
Florida fig, live oak, and some palm species have aggressive root systems that seek moisture from sewer lines. Any sewer line within 20 feet of these trees should be scoped every 3–5 years. Warning signs: recurring slow drains, frequent main-line clogs, gurgling sounds from multiple fixtures simultaneously.
🟢 P-Trap Mold (Unused Drains)
Florida's average relative humidity (74% RH) creates ideal mold conditions at dry p-traps in guest bathrooms and seldom-used sinks. Run water for 30 seconds weekly in all unused fixtures. Pour one cup of diluted bleach monthly into floor drains and infrequently used floor drain p-traps.
🌿 Irrigation & Water District Rules
St. Johns, South Florida, and Southwest Florida Water Management Districts restrict irrigation to 2 designated days per week year-round. Adjust your controller each season — overwatering saturates soil, which stresses underground service connections and can accelerate joint degradation in older PVC lines.
🚨 Emergency Prep: Know Your Shutoffs
Main Water Valve
Near the street meter box. The utility side is the utility's responsibility — the home side is yours. Know the difference before a leak starts.
Toilet Shutoff
Oval knob behind/below the tank. Turn clockwise to close. Test annually — a stiff valve seizes and fails in real emergencies.
Under-Sink Valves
Hot and cold angle stops under each sink cabinet. Turn clockwise to shut off. Lubricate annually so they don't freeze in place.
Water Heater
Cold water inlet valve at the top of tank. Also locate the T&P relief valve — it's a critical safety device, not just a fitting.
📸 Pro Tip: Photograph each shutoff location and save the images labeled in your phone contacts (e.g., "HOUSE — Main Water Shutoff"). In a leak emergency, every second counts.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does maintenance plan cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida maintenance plan estimates run about $2,000-$5,000, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Many common jobs are completed the same day, while larger projects can take longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm a timeline after assessing your home.
