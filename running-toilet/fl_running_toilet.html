The Dye Test (Confirm a Leak in 5 Minutes)

Easy field test: Drop a few drops of food coloring (or a dye tablet) into the toilet tank. Do NOT flush. Wait 15 minutes. If color appears in the bowl without flushing, water is escaping through the flapper. This is the most common running toilet cause.
  1. Lift the tank lid. Add 5–10 drops of food coloring (any dark color).
  2. Do NOT flush. Wait exactly 15 minutes without disturbing the toilet.
  3. Look at the bowl water — did color appear? If YES: flapper is leaking.
  4. If no color in bowl but toilet still sounds like it runs: the fill valve is leaking water over the overflow tube.
  5. To confirm fill valve overflow: remove tank lid and look at the overflow tube. Water level should be 1" below the top. If water is spilling over the tube, the fill valve is the problem.

Diagnose by Symptom

DIY Fix
🟦 Phantom Flush (Refills on Its Own)
Toilet randomly refills even though nobody used it — a gurgling or running sound that starts and stops. This is almost always a leaking flapper allowing tank water to slowly drain into the bowl until the fill valve activates.

Confirm: Dye test (above) — color will appear in bowl within 15 minutes.

Typical cause: Flapper worn, warped by FL hard water minerals, or buildup on flapper seat preventing full seal.

Fix: Replace flapper. Universal flappers ($5–15) fit most toilets. Toto and Kohler recommends brand-specific flappers for better fit in FL hard water.
DIY Fix
🟦 Constant Hissing / Running Sound
Toilet hisses continuously. Usually means water is entering faster than it drains, or the fill valve is not shutting off completely.

Possible causes: (1) Fill valve worn and not closing fully — hear hiss at tank, not bowl. (2) Float set too high — water level at or above overflow tube. (3) Fill valve debris from FL sediment blocking shut-off.

Fix: Adjust float to lower water level. If that doesn't stop the hiss, replace the fill valve ($10–$25 part, 20-minute install).
May Need Plumber
🟨 Water Visible Running Into Bowl
Can see water visibly moving at the back of the bowl even at rest. High-volume leak, 500–4,000+ gal/day.

Most likely cause: Severely worn flapper, cracked flush valve seat, or fill valve fully stuck open.

Check: Flush valve seat for cracks or mineral buildup. Run finger around the seat rim. If rough, it needs to be sanded (Fluidmaster seat grinder) or the entire flush valve assembly replaced.

Fix: Flush valve seat repair ($8 kit) or full flush valve replacement ($15–$45 part + $175–$275 plumber labor).
Plumber Recommended
🟥 Fill Valve Runs After Full Tank
Tank fills but the running sound continues. Water level is below the overflow tube.

Likely cause: Fill valve diaphragm or seal worn. Common in FL hard water as mineral deposits damage the diaphragm seat. The valve cannot fully close.

Fix: Replace fill valve. Korky or Fluidmaster fill valves designed for FL hard water are available at hardware stores. DIY: $10–$25. Plumber: $150–$225 total.
DIY Check
🟦 Toilet Rocks or Base Leaks After Flush
Not a running toilet, but a water leak at the base after flushing indicates a failed wax ring. This is separate from internal running toilet issues and should not be ignored — it can cause subfloor damage and drain odors.

Signs: Water on floor after flushing, rocking toilet, sewer odor at base, stained caulk.

Fix: Wax ring replacement by a plumber ($175–$350). Do not DIY unless you are comfortable with toilet removal and resetting.
FL Hard Water Issue
🟨 Flapper Deteriorates Every 1–2 Years
If you replace your flapper frequently (every 1–2 years), FL hard water is destroying the rubber. FL water averages 10–20 GPG hardness (very hard), and the calcium carbonate attacks rubber flappers.

Long-term fix: Switch to a Fluidmaster 501, Korky 100BP, or Toto Flapperless system designed for hard water. Consider a tank tablet water softener insert or a whole-house water softener upstream.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does running toilet cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida running toilet estimates run about $325-$950, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Many common jobs are completed the same day, while larger projects can take longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm a timeline after assessing your home.
