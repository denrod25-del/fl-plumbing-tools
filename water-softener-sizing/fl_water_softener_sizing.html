💎 Why Florida Needs a Larger Softener

FL water is among the hardest in the US. The Floridan Aquifer naturally dissolves calcium and magnesium from limestone as water percolates down — most FL counties average 15–25 GPG (grains per gallon). That's 3–5x the "soft" threshold of 3.5 GPG. The formula: size your softener on grain capacity, not just household size. FL homes consistently need larger tanks than equivalent-size homes in other states.

How the Formula Works
Daily Grains = People × GPD × GPG
Weekly Grains = Daily × 7
Tank Size = Weekly Grains ÷ Salt Efficiency

Salt Efficiency: Standard resin = 3,333 gr/lb
High-efficiency resin = 4,000 gr/lb

⚙️ Standard softener efficiency: regenerates once/week using ~6–8 lbs of salt per 1,000 grains capacity. High-efficiency (demand-initiated regeneration) saves 30–50% on salt usage — important for FL septic owners.

Salt-Free (TAC) Alternatives
🔬Template Assisted Crystallization (TAC): Salt-free water conditioners don't remove hardness minerals — they convert dissolved calcium/magnesium into harmless microscopic crystals that won't form scale. Result: no scale buildup on pipes, fixtures, and appliances, but water tests as "hard" (still high GPG) and won't produce lather like truly softened water. No salt, no electricity, no wastewater from regeneration. Popular for FL septic owners wanting scale prevention without sodium discharge.
⚠️TAC Limitations in FL: TAC is not effective above 25 GPG hardness — many FL counties exceed this. TAC does not treat iron — if FL well water contains iron, TAC must be preceded by iron removal. TAC is not a "softener" — won't give soap lather improvement, won't prevent soap scum, won't leave skin feeling soft. It is a scale preventer only. For full softening benefits (skin, soap, appliances) in FL, traditional ion exchange (salt-based) is required.
