📜 FL Statute 381.0065 — Septic Authority
▼
Florida Statute 381.0065 grants the Florida Department of Health (FDOH) authority over onsite sewage treatment and disposal systems (OSTDS). Key provisions:

• All OSTDS must be permitted and inspected by the county health department before installation.
• Permits are required for new systems, repair, modification, and abandonment.
• Systems must comply with FAC 64E-6 minimum standards.
• Counties may impose stricter requirements in their jurisdictions.
• FDOH maintains statewide inspection and enforcement authority.
• Penalty for operating without a permit: civil fine up to $5,000 per day.

🔄 FS 381.00655 — Mandatory Pump-Out
▼
FL law requires septic tanks to be pumped-out at minimum every 5 years. Key facts:

• Applies to all OSTDS statewide.
• New-home exemption: first pump-out due within 5 years of installation date.
• Owner must retain pump-out records for at least 5 years.
• Some counties (e.g., Pinellas, Indian River, Sarasota) enforce more frequently.
• Failing to comply can result in a Notice of Violation from the county health dept.
• Cost: typically $250–$450 for standard residential tank pump-out.
🏗️ Permit Process — Step by Step
▼
1
Site Evaluation — Hire a FL-licensed soil evaluator or engineer. Soil borings, water table depth, and site plan required. Cost: $300–$600.
2
Application Submission — Submit FDOH Form DH 4015 to the county health department with site plan, soil evaluation, and permit fee.
3
Plan Review — County reviews for code compliance. Typically 5–15 business days. Additional info may be requested.
4
Permit Issued — Permit valid for 18 months. Includes approved design, required inspections, and permit number.
5
Installation — Only FL-licensed septic contractors (CPSC or MRC) may install. Inspections required at each phase.
6
Final Inspection — County inspector verifies installation matches approved plans. Certificate of Completion issued.
7
Operating Permit — County issues operating permit after passing inspection. System legally operable.
🌊 Environmentally Sensitive Areas
▼
Certain FL areas require advanced treatment systems or prohibit OSTDS entirely:

• Outstanding Florida Waters (OFW): 100 ft drain field setback, PBTS may be required.
• Springs Protection Areas: Advanced N-removal systems required near springs.
• Florida Keys (Monroe Co.): Stringent nitrogen-removal requirements; nutrient-reducing systems mandated.
• Aquifer Protection Zones: Enhanced protection requirements in recharge areas.
• WBID Impaired Waters: Nutrient TMDL compliance may require system upgrades.
• Coastal setback lines: Systems near coastal construction control lines require DEP coordination.

Always check FDEP Oceans & Coastal Management and WMD maps before selecting a site.
🔑 System Abandonment Requirements
▼
When connecting to public sewer or replacing a system:

• An OSTDS abandonment permit is required from the county health dept.
• Tank must be pumped out by a licensed contractor.
• Tank must be crushed, filled with clean sand/gravel, or removed entirely.
• Drain field pipes must be removed or crushed.
• Certificate of abandonment issued by county health dept.
• Cost: $300–$800 for abandonment permit + contractor fee.
• Do NOT abandon a system without a permit — fines up to $5,000.
