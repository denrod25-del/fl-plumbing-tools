Why Florida is the Best US State for Tankless
🌡️FL Groundwater Temperature Advantage: Florida's Floridan Aquifer maintains a groundwater temperature of 70–75°F year-round (vs. 37–55°F in northern states). This is the single biggest factor in tankless performance: a 40,000 BTU unit that produces 2.0 GPM in Minnesota produces 4.5–5.0 GPM in Florida, because the required temperature rise is half. FL homeowners get more hot water from smaller, cheaper units.
💰Energy Savings — Bigger in FL: Standard tank water heaters lose heat through the tank walls constantly (standby losses). In FL's heat, the tank's surroundings are warmer (garage temps 85–100°F in summer) — but the tank still loses 20-40% of energy to standby losses. Tankless heats on demand only. Average FL household: $200–$500/year savings on water heating vs. standard tank. In all-electric FL homes, water heating is typically 15-20% of electric bill — tankless reduces this significantly.
🏠Space Savings — Critical in FL: FL homes often have small utility closets or garage-mounted water heaters. Tankless units are wall-mounted, roughly the size of a small suitcase (20"×14"×9"), replacing a 40–80 gallon tank that occupies significant floor space. In FL's cramped garages, this space recapture is significant. Outdoor installation possible in FL's warm climate (exterior wall mount) — eliminates any interior space use entirely.
🌊No Flood Loss — Hurricane Resilience: FL homeowners with garage-mounted tank water heaters lose them in flooding events (Hurricane Irma, Ian, Idalia all caused water heater losses). Tankless units mounted high on garage walls or exterior walls are above typical FL flood levels. After Hurricane Ian (2022), tankless units mounted at 5+ feet survived while tank water heaters on garage floors were total losses.
♻️FL Utility Rebates — Available: Several FL utilities offer rebates for high-efficiency water heater replacement: FPL — $50–$100 for ENERGY STAR certified tankless. Duke Energy FL — $100–$200 for qualifying gas tankless. TECO (Tampa Electric) — rebate program varies. Gainesville Regional Utilities — up to $200. Always verify current rebate at utility's website — programs change annually.
FL Hard Water Warning — Scale Is Tankless Enemy #1
🪨FL Hard Water Reality: Central and South FL has some of the hardest water in the US — 15–25 grains per gallon (GPG) in many areas (anything over 7 GPG is "hard"). Calcium carbonate scales deposit inside the tankless heat exchanger, progressively reducing efficiency and flow. An unprotected tankless in FL hard water may need descaling every 12–18 months; a properly conditioned system descales every 3–5 years.
🛡️Scale Prevention Options: (1) Water softener (salt-based ion exchange) — most effective but adds sodium to water; requires backwash drain. (2) TAC (Template Assisted Crystallization) — salt-free, changes calcium crystal form so it doesn't adhere to surfaces. No drain needed. Most FL tankless installers now recommend TAC system pre-filter. (3) Phosphate feeder — inexpensive, adds trace phosphate that coats surfaces; EPA-approved for potable water at low doses. (4) Descaling service — annual citric acid flush if no prevention system.
📋Warranty Implications: Most tankless manufacturers (Rinnai, Navien, Noritz) VOID the heat exchanger warranty for scale damage if water hardness exceeds 11 GPG without documented water treatment. Get your FL water hardness tested (free via county extension office or home test kit $15) before installation. If hardness is 11+ GPG, document your water treatment system for warranty protection.
FL Permit & Code Requirements
📋Permit Required: FL Building Code requires permit for water heater replacement (tankless or tank). Gas tankless additionally requires gas line inspection. Many FL counties have online permit portals — licensed contractors pull permits as standard practice. DIY water heater installation without permit is illegal in FL and voids homeowner's insurance coverage for related damage.
📏Venting Requirements: Direct vent (sealed combustion) gas tankless: requires Category III or IV stainless vent pipe — not standard PVC. Some FL models use CPVC vent for condensing units. Vent must terminate minimum 12" above any air intake, 12" above grade, 1" from any combustibles. FL's coastal areas: use stainless (not aluminum) vent components — salt air corrodes aluminum.
🔥Gas Sizing: High-output gas tankless (199,000 BTU) requires ¾" or 1" gas line depending on run length. Most FL homes have ½" gas supply to water heater location — upsizing required at $300–$900 depending on run length. Gas meter upsizing may be required — utility service involvement.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does tankless sizing cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida tankless sizing estimates run about $1,200-$3,500, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, gas work, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Many common jobs are completed the same day, while larger projects can take longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm a timeline after assessing your home.
