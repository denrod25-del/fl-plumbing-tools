A water heater is easy to take for granted — until the hot water turns rusty, the tank starts rumbling, or you find a puddle in the garage. In hard-water Jupiter, that day can arrive sooner than expected, because scale and sediment quietly wear a heater down from the inside.

Why this matters in Jupiter

Palm Beach County water tends to be hard, and Jupiter is no exception. Inside a tank water heater, that hardness precipitates out as scale and sediment — coating the bottom of the tank and the heating element or burner area. The result is a unit that works harder, runs less efficiently, and corrodes faster. A heater that might last 10 to 12 years on softened water can be pushed toward early failure, with hard-water tanks often reaching only about 7 to 9 years.

The replacement + softener bundle

The single most effective way to protect a new water heater in Jupiter is to address the water that feeds it. Many owners replace the heater and add a water softener at the same time, so the new unit starts life shielded from scale. It’s also typically more efficient to do both in one visit than to come back later — and it helps the new heater reach the upper end of its expected life.

Signs your water heater needs replacing

  • Rusty, discolored, or metallic-smelling hot water
  • Popping, rumbling, or crackling sounds (sediment cooking at the bottom)
  • Water that doesn’t get as hot, or runs out faster than it used to
  • Moisture, rust, or corrosion around the base or fittings
  • Age beyond roughly 8 to 10 years
  • Any active leak from the tank itself — replace promptly

Your options

Tank water heater

A like-for-like tank replacement is the lowest upfront cost and the simplest swap. Modern tanks are more efficient than older units, and with softened water they can reach the upper end of their service life. Available in electric and gas.

Tankless water heater

A tankless (on-demand) unit costs more to install but frees up space, can last longer, and delivers continuous hot water. Hard water still matters here: tankless heat exchangers need periodic descaling and benefit from a softener as well. If you’re weighing a switch, a plumber can compare it against a tank for your household.

Add a water softener

Whichever heater you choose, a softener removes the calcium and magnesium that drive scale, protecting not just the heater but your fixtures, dishwasher, and plumbing throughout the home.

What affects the cost

  • Tank vs. tankless and electric vs. gas
  • Capacity / sizing for your household’s hot-water demand
  • Whether you add a water softener at the same time
  • Code upgrades — pan, expansion tank, or venting changes
  • Relocation if the unit moves
  • Permit and inspection fees through your local building department

Permits & who can do the work

Water heater replacement is permitted work in Florida. In Jupiter the permit and inspection run through the Town of Jupiter building department; nearby unincorporated areas fall under Palm Beach County’s building division (the “authority having jurisdiction,” or AHJ). Work should be performed by a Florida state-licensed plumbing contractor, who normally pulls the permit and schedules the inspection. Adding a softener may be a separate line of permitted work. Confirm current permit fees and requirements with your specific AHJ — they vary and change over time.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a water heater last in Jupiter?

A conventional tank water heater commonly lasts around 8 to 12 years, but hard water can shorten that. In hard-water areas it’s not unusual to see tanks reach only about 7 to 9 years before scale and sediment take their toll.

Pairing a heater with a water softener is one of the most effective ways to protect its life.

Why does hard water shorten water heater life?

Hard water deposits scale and sediment inside the tank and on the heating element or burner area. That buildup insulates the water from the heat source, makes the unit work harder, and accelerates corrosion — all of which can bring on early failure.

Softening the water reduces the scale that drives this wear.

Should I replace the heater and add a softener at the same time?

Many Jupiter owners do exactly that. Installing a new water heater is a natural moment to add a softener, so the new unit starts life protected from scale. Bundling the work can also be more efficient than two separate visits. A licensed plumber can quote both together.

Tank or tankless for my Jupiter home?

Tank heaters are lower upfront cost and simple to replace like-for-like. Tankless units cost more to install but save space, can last longer, and provide on-demand hot water.

Either way, hard water matters — tankless units need periodic descaling and benefit from a softener too. A plumber can compare the options for your household.

What are the signs my water heater is failing?

Watch for rusty or discolored hot water, popping or rumbling sounds from sediment, water that no longer gets as hot or runs out quickly, moisture or rust around the base, and age beyond about 8 to 10 years.

Any leaking from the tank itself means it’s time to replace promptly.

Do I need a permit to replace a water heater in Jupiter?

Yes — water heater replacement is permitted work. Your licensed plumbing contractor typically pulls the permit through the Town of Jupiter building department (or Palm Beach County for unincorporated areas) and schedules inspection. Verify current fees with your AHJ.
