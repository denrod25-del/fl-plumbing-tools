Florida Bathroom Remodel Guide

Eight essential sections covering FL code, waterproofing, permits, valves, fans, and trade sequencing.

1. FL Bathroom Remodel Permit Requirements by Scope

Florida building permit requirements for bathroom remodels follow the Florida Building Code (FBC) strictly.

NO PERMIT Not Required in FL

  • Replacing fixtures in same location (toilet, faucet, showerhead, mirror, vanity light) — same holes, same connections
  • Painting, flooring over existing (no subfloor work), cabinet hardware replacement

PERMIT Plumbing Permit Required

  • Any new rough-in (moving drain, supply, or vent locations)
  • Tub-to-shower conversion — requires new drain location and waterproof liner (triggers permit)
  • New bathroom addition
  • Water heater replacement in bathroom (separate permit)

REQUIRED Building + Plumbing Permit

  • Structural wall changes or removal
  • New window or skylight
  • Adding exhaust fan where none existed (electrical permit also required)
  • Floor system modification (subfloor repair/replacement)
  • Adding square footage
FL practical rule: if you are changing where anything drains or where supply lines connect — pull a permit. FL permits protect you: if unpermitted bathroom work is discovered during home sale (very common in FL), remediation can cost $5,000–20,000 and kill the deal.
2. FL Wet Area Waterproofing: The Non-Negotiable

FL's humidity makes bathroom waterproofing more critical than almost anywhere in the country. FBC Section R307 and ANSI A108 standards govern FL shower waterproofing.

Traditional Mortar Bed with CPE Liner

Fiberglass or PVC liner (40-mil minimum) set in sloped mortar bed. FL standard since the 1970s. Liner must be fully embedded, drain clamped to liner. Lifespan: 15–25 years. Cost: $400–800 for standard shower. Most common FL master bath tile shower construction.

Sheet Membrane (Schluter Kerdi, Wedi)

Fabric or foam membrane bonded to substrate, joints sealed with Kerdi-band. Fast installation (1–2 days vs 3–5 for mortar bed). Lightweight. FL inspectors increasingly familiar. Lifespan: 20–30 years. Cost: $600–1,200. Excellent for FL renovations over existing subfloor.

Liquid-Applied Membrane (RedGard, HydroBar, Laticrete Hydro Ban)

Brush/roll-applied waterproofing over cement board or existing surface. Most economical. FL inspectors vary on acceptance — confirm locally. Must achieve specified mil thickness (minimum 60-mil for FL wet areas). Lifespan: 10–20 years. Cost: $200–500 materials.

FL mold tip: regardless of waterproofing system, use only epoxy grout in FL showers. Cement grout absorbs water and molds within 1–3 years in FL humidity. Epoxy grout costs $80–200 more in materials but lasts 20+ years without mold.
3. FL Tub-to-Shower Conversion: The Most Common FL Remodel

The bathtub-to-walk-in-shower conversion is the single most common FL bathroom remodel. FL climate, aging population (FL #1 for 65+ population), and rental/resale value all drive this trend.

FL Plumbing Requirements for Tub-to-Shower Conversion

  • Drain relocation: Tub drain (typically 14″–18″ from wall) must move to shower center or off-center position. Requires cutting the concrete slab in most FL homes. Slab cut cost: $400–800.
  • Valve replacement: Tub/shower diverter valve replaced with dedicated shower valve. Use pressure-balance (ASSE 1016) or thermostatic valve (ASSE 1070).
  • Waterproofing: Full shower waterproofing system required — most important step for FL.
  • Shower door/enclosure: Frameless glass is FL standard ($800–2,500). Tempered safety glass per FL code required.
  • Permit: Required in FL — drain relocation = slab penetration = permit mandatory.

4. FL Shower Valve Selection for Remodels

Valve selection is the most consequential plumbing decision in a FL bathroom remodel — it affects comfort, safety, and reliability for 15–25 years.

Pressure-Balance Valve (ASSE 1016)

Required by FL code. Maintains water temperature when pressure changes (toilet flush, other fixtures). Single control for temperature and volume. FL standard: Moen Posi-Temp, Delta Monitor, Kohler Rite-Temp. Cost installed: $350–600.

Thermostatic Valve (ASSE 1070)

Maintains exact temperature set point regardless of pressure or incoming water temperature variation. FL benefit: FL hot water varies seasonally — thermostatic provides consistent shower temp. FL brands: Rohl, Hansgrohe, Grohe, Kohler DTV. Cost installed: $700–2,500+.

Volume Control Separated from Thermostatic

Allows temperature to be pre-set; separate handle controls flow. Common in luxury FL master baths. Allows body sprays/rainhead on separate volume controls from main showerhead. Code: thermostatic + pressure balance in series for FL compliance.

FL hard water note: specify ceramic disc cartridges in all FL shower valves. Rubber cartridges fail in 2–4 years in FL hard water vs. 10–15 years for ceramic disc. Request OEM ceramic cartridge documentation from your plumber.
5. FL Exhaust Fan Requirements in Bathroom Remodels

FL bathroom exhaust fans are not optional — FBC Section M1507 requires mechanical ventilation in bathrooms without operable exterior windows.

  • Minimum exhaust rate: 50 CFM for intermittent, 20 CFM for continuous
  • For FL showers: HVI recommends 1 CFM per sq ft of shower area, minimum 50 CFM for standard shower
  • Steam showers: 200+ CFM required for FL (steam generation + FL humidity = extreme moisture load)
  • Duct to exterior: FL code requires exhaust duct to terminate at exterior. No attic termination (common FL code violation — creates serious mold in FL attics)
  • FL recommended fans: Panasonic WhisperCeiling, Broan-NuTone 688, Delta BreezSmart (humidity sensing)
  • Humidity sensing: strongly recommended for FL — fan activates when humidity rises and runs until humidity returns to setpoint

6. Florida Trade Sequencing for Bathroom Remodel

FL bathroom remodel requires careful trade coordination. Standard FL sequence for full gut remodel:

Week 1

  • Day 1: Demo — remove existing fixtures, tile, drywall/cement board, flooring
  • Day 2: Plumber rough-in — relocate/add drain lines, supply lines, vent modifications. Inspector called for rough-in inspection.
  • Day 3: Electrician rough-in — GFCI outlets, exhaust fan wiring, lighting rough-in. Inspector called.

Week 2

  • Day 4–5: Plumbing rough-in inspection passed. Cement board/Wedi installation. Waterproofing application + cure.
  • Day 6: Tile installation begins (floor first, then walls)
  • Day 7: Tile completion, grout — epoxy grout preferred for FL

Week 3

  • Day 8: Plumber finish — install fixtures (toilet, faucet, shower valve trim, shower pan drain)
  • Day 9: Electrician finish — outlets, fan, lighting
  • Day 10: Glass enclosure installation
  • Day 11: Final inspection (building + plumbing + electrical)
  • Day 12: Touch-up, punch list
FL common delays: permit (1–5 days), inspection scheduling (2–5 days), tile special order (2–3 weeks), custom glass door (2–4 weeks). Plan FL bathroom remodel timeline: 4–8 weeks total from permit to final inspection for standard remodel.
7. FL Master Bath Walk-In Shower Design: Plumbing Considerations

Large FL master bath showers (common in FL luxury market and primary residence builds) have specific plumbing requirements.

Multi-Head Systems

Combining rainhead + body sprays + handheld requires: (a) thermostatic mixing valve with multiple volume controls for each outlet, (b) pressure booster pump if home pressure is under 60 PSI with multiple heads running simultaneously, (c) minimum 3/4″ supply line to shower valve (not 1/2″ as used in standard showers). FL master bath standard: 3/4″ supply from main, 1/2″ feeds to individual valves.

Minimum Shower Size

FL code follows IRC: 30″ × 30″ minimum. ADA accessible shower (increasingly required in FL rental properties and aging-in-place remodels): 36″ × 36″ minimum, 60″ × 36″ for roll-in. Zero-threshold entry: FL standard for luxury and accessible showers. Requires linear drain (Schluter Kerdi-Line) or point drain with proper slope in all directions.

Curbless Shower in FL

Excellent choice for FL — no curb = no water retention = better drainage. Requires careful slope planning (minimum 1/4″ per foot toward drain) and waterproof system extending 12″+ beyond shower area onto bathroom floor.

Digital Shower Controls

Kohler DTV+, Moen U, Hansgrohe ShowerSelect — programmable temperature and flow. FL consideration: WiFi connectivity in humid bathroom environment — ensure IP44 minimum rating. FL power reliability (storms) can require backup settings.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does bathroom remodel plumbing cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida bathroom remodel plumbing estimates run about $50,000-$150,000, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Timelines depend on scope - many routine jobs take a few hours to a day, while larger projects run longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm after assessing your home.
