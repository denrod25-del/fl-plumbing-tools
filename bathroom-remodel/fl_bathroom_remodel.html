⚠ Slab Foundation — Concrete Work Required

Moving drains in a slab home requires jackhammering concrete — this adds $500–$2,500+ per drain location and requires a plumbing permit in Florida. Budget an additional $300–$800 for concrete patching and allow 3–5 days for curing before flooring can be installed. All slab plumbing work in Florida requires a permit and rough-in inspection before concrete is poured back.

Florida-Specific Considerations
💧 Mold, Moisture & Waterproofing

Florida's year-round humidity (average 74–80% RH) makes bathroom waterproofing a code requirement, not just best practice. FL Building Code Section 1203.4 mandates mechanical exhaust ventilation in all bathrooms — minimum 50 CFM fan ducted to exterior, or operable window to outdoors.

Required backing materials: Cement board (e.g., Durock, HardieBacker) or glass mat (e.g., DensShield) is mandatory behind all wet-area tile. Standard drywall will fail within 12–24 months in FL humidity — inspectors may flag this violation.

Shower pan waterproofing: Consider Schluter Kerdi, WEDI, or USG Durock shower systems for a fully bonded waterproofing layer. Hot-mopped liners are also common in FL but require a separate skilled trade. RedGard liquid membrane is an economical option for budget-conscious projects.

🔩 Hard Water & Mineral Buildup

Most FL municipalities supply hard water (8–14 GPG in Miami, Tampa, and Orlando metro areas). Hard water accelerates valve cartridge wear, clogs aerators, and stains fixtures — significantly reducing the lifespan of new plumbing investments.

Before installing new fixtures: Verify your home has a Pressure Reducing Valve (PRV) — target 55–65 PSI. High pressure (above 80 PSI) is common in FL and dramatically shortens fixture life. Consider a whole-house sediment filter and water softener before committing to high-end fixtures.

Fixture selection tip: In hard-water areas, specify ceramic disc cartridges (Moen, Delta, Kohler) and solid brass valve bodies — they outlast zinc/plastic alternatives by 3–5x in FL conditions.

🌀 Hurricane & Impact Window Considerations

Before planning new plumbing wall locations, check your existing hurricane shutter tracks, impact window frames, and accordion shutter swing paths. Plumbing walls built in a shutter track zone will require shutter relocation — an expensive add-on.

Coastal flood zone (A/AE/VE) requirements: If your home is in a FEMA flood zone, plumbing supply lines and certain drain cleanouts may have minimum elevation requirements. Verify with your local building department before rough-in work begins.

Post-storm assessment: After major storms, check supply line connections and P-traps — vibration from nearby impacts can loosen compression fittings and shift unsupported PVC runs.

    ⚠ Florida Statute 489.147 — Unlicensed Contractor Warning

    Florida Statute 489.147 makes it a felony to knowingly hire an unlicensed contractor for work that requires a license. Always verify your plumber's state license at myfloridalicense.com (search "plumbing" under Contractor). Unlicensed work: (1) voids your homeowner's insurance for related claims, (2) may require full tear-out at your expense, (3) makes your home unsellable until violations are corrected, and (4) creates personal liability if a worker is injured on your property.

    Inspection Stages — Why They Protect You
    🔍 Florida Plumbing Inspection Stages

    Florida requires two inspection stages for all permitted plumbing work:

    Stage 1 — Rough-In Inspection: Required before walls are closed or concrete is poured. The inspector verifies: drain location and slope (min. 1/4" per foot), vent stack connections, pressure test (min. 10 PSI for 15 minutes), and proper pipe support. Never close a wall or pour concrete before this inspection passes. Doing so requires demolition for re-inspection.

    Stage 2 — Final Inspection: Required after all fixtures are installed but before the bathroom is used. Verifies fixture installation, proper water pressure, drain flow rate, no active leaks, and ventilation compliance (fan or operable window).

    Why this protects you at resale: Passed inspections create a public record in your county's permit system. Buyers and their inspectors can verify all work was done legally. Uninspected plumbing can trigger insurance claim denials on related water damage and may require full replacement as a condition of sale.

    Permit in your name: In Florida, the permit should be pulled in the homeowner's name — not the contractor's. This ensures you retain the permit record and can verify inspection status independently. A contractor who insists on pulling the permit in their name may be obscuring issues.

      Florida Quick Answers

      How much does bathroom remodel cost in Florida?

      On this page, Florida bathroom remodel estimates run about $500-$2,500, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

      What affects the price?

      Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

      Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

      In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

      Does homeowners insurance cover it?

      It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

      How long does it take?

      Timelines depend on scope - many routine jobs take a few hours to a day, while larger projects run longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm after assessing your home.
