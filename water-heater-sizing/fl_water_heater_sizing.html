FL-Specific Pros & Cons

Storage Tank — FL Considerations
PRO Lower upfront cost — important in FL's high COL markets
PRO Simpler installation — no FL gas line upgrade needed for electric
PRO Works fine in FL garage — mild temps protect tank
CON FL hard water accelerates anode rod depletion — inspect annually

CON 8–12 year lifespan in hard water FL areas
Tankless — FL Considerations
PRO FL warm inlet water means SMALLER unit required → lower cost vs. other states
PRO 15–20 year lifespan (with annual descaling for FL hard water)
PRO Never runs cold — great for FL vacation rentals and large families
CON FL hard water causes scale buildup — must descale annually
CON Gas models may require FL gas line upgrade ($400–$1,200)
NOTE Electric tankless requires 240V circuit — check panel capacity

🌡️ FL Heat Pump (Hybrid) — The Smart Choice

Florida's warm climate makes hybrid heat pump water heaters extremely efficient. The heat pump extracts heat from ambient air (your garage or utility closet) and transfers it to water — using 60–70% less electricity than standard electric tanks. FL's year-round warm air temps make heat pump mode viable 12 months/year (unlike northern states where units switch to resistance heat in winter).

Energy Factor (EF)3.5–4.0 (vs 0.9 for standard electric)
Annual savings vs standard electric$350–$550/year in FL
FL average payback period4–6 years
FPL Rebate availableUp to $200
Federal tax credit (IRA 2022)30% of cost, up to $600
FL garage suitabilityExcellent — warm air year-round

⚡ FL Electric vs Gas Cost Comparison

🔌 FL Electric Utility Rates (2025)

FPL — Florida Power & Light

SE FL: Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach | SW FL: Lee, Collier, Charlotte
~$0.1198/kWh (residential, includes fuel charge)

FL's largest utility, 5.7 million customers. FPL offers Heat Pump WH rebate ($200) and On-Bill Financing for ENERGY STAR appliances. No time-of-use rate for most residential customers. FPL On-Bill Financing: 0% interest up to $5,000 for qualifying energy efficiency upgrades.

Duke Energy Florida

Central FL: Orange, Seminole, Polk, Pasco, Citrus, Hernando
~$0.1285/kWh (residential energy charge + fuel charge)

Duke offers EV charging and home energy programs. Heat pump WH rebate available through Smart $aver program ($100–$300). Duke's Green Source program available for renewable energy offset. Citrus and Marion County customers have slightly different rate structure.

TECO (Tampa Electric)

Hillsborough County and portions of Polk, Pasco, Pinellas
~$0.1142/kWh (one of FL's lower electric rates)

TECO Energy Efficiency Program offers appliance rebates including water heaters. TECO's lower rate makes electric water heating cost-competitive vs. gas in Tampa area. Smart Energy program includes time-of-use options for customers with smart meters.

JEA — Jacksonville Electric Authority

Duval County and portions of Nassau, Clay, St. Johns
~$0.1265/kWh (residential blended rate)

JEA is a community-owned utility. JEA offers rebates for ENERGY STAR certified heat pump water heaters. JEA's sustainability initiative includes GoGreen programs. Jacksonville's lower cost of living means water heating a smaller percentage of household budget.

🔥 FL Gas Utility Options

Peoples Gas (now Emera Florida)

Hillsborough, Pinellas, Manatee, Sarasota, Polk, Orange, Brevard + more
~$1.42/therm (residential, includes distribution + supply)

Largest natural gas utility in FL. Gas water heating cost-competitive with electric in Peoples Gas service area. Peoples Gas ENERGIZE program offers rebates for high-efficiency gas appliances. 40-gallon gas tank costs approximately $360–$500/year in fuel.

Florida City Gas (now Chesapeake Utilities)

Miami-Dade, Broward, Indian River, St. Lucie, Martin counties
~$1.55/therm (residential)

Serves Southeast FL where natural gas availability is limited compared to other regions. Gas line installation cost $800–$2,500 if not already present. Gas water heating rebates available for high-efficiency units (EF ≥ 0.70).

Propane (Rural & Island FL)

Rural counties: Highlands, Glades, Hardee, Okeechobee, DeSoto + rural areas statewide
$2.80–$3.80/gallon (FL market rate, 2025)

Propane used where natural gas service unavailable. Propane tankless heaters very popular in rural FL and Keys communities. Annual propane cost for water heating: $480–$820 for average household. Bulk propane contracts reduce per-gallon cost 15–25%.

💰 FL Rebates & Incentives 2025

Federal Tax Credit (IRA 2022)

30% of cost, up to $600 — Heat Pump WH

Applies to heat pump water heaters with Energy Factor ≥ 2.0. Claimed on Form 5695. Available through 2032. Electric panel upgrades needed for heat pump WH may also qualify (up to $600 additional).

FPL Heat Pump Water Heater Rebate

$200 rebate for ENERGY STAR certified heat pump WH

Requires purchase of qualifying ENERGY STAR heat pump water heater. FPL customers in SE and SW FL. Must submit within 90 days of installation. Available to residential customers only.

Duke Energy Smart $aver Program

$100–$300 depending on unit type

Heat pump water heaters qualify for highest rebate tier. Central FL Duke customers. Rebate processed within 6–8 weeks. Additional rebates available for EV chargers and smart thermostats when combined with WH rebate.

FL PACE Financing (Property Assessed Clean Energy)

100% financing, repaid through property tax — no upfront cost

Available in most FL counties for energy-efficient water heaters including heat pump and solar. PACE providers in FL: Ygrene, Renew Financial, Florida PACE Funding Agency. No minimum credit score. Loan stays with property if sold.
