⚡ Pressure Tank Sizing Calculator

An undersized pressure tank causes short-cycling — the pump turns on and off rapidly, destroying the motor. In FL's hot climate, a short-cycling pump fails 2–3× faster than properly sized systems.

🔍 FL Pressure System Diagnostics

⚡ Short Cycling (Pump clicking on/off rapidly)

Most common FL well complaint

Waterlogged pressure tank — bladder or diaphragm has failed, tank lost air charge. In FL's high humidity, tank corrosion accelerates. Replace pressure tank. Typical FL tank life: 8–12 years in coastal areas, 12–18 years inland.

Urgency: HighFix: Tank replacement

📉 Low Water Pressure Throughout Home

Check pressure switch, tank precharge, and pump output

Could be: undersized pressure switch, partially failed pump impeller, clogged well screen (common with FL sandy soil), low aquifer yield in drought conditions, or waterlogged tank. FL summer drought June–October can drop well yield significantly.

Urgency: MediumNeeds diagnosis

🔴 No Water / Pump Won't Prime

Emergency — call immediately

Pump failure, loss of prime (jet pump), burned motor winding (submersible), or broken pitless adapter. In FL lightning storms, pump motors are a common lightning strike casualty — surge protection is a must. Submersible pump replacement is a specialized job requiring well service equipment.

Urgency: EmergencyPossible motor failure

🌊 Sputtering / Air in Lines

Air entering suction side

Possible low water table (FL drought), broken well screen allowing air entry, or failing pitless adapter. FL's dry season (Nov–Apr) drops water table 3–8 feet in many areas, temporarily reducing pump yield and allowing air entry. Often resolves with rain but should be inspected.

Urgency: ModerateSeasonal or structural

🧪 FL Well Water Quality Checker

Florida well water ranges from excellent to severely contaminated depending on region and aquifer. Select the symptoms you're experiencing for a likely diagnosis and treatment recommendation.

🟤 Red/brown staining on sinks, toilets, laundryHigh iron content — extremely common in FL Floridan aquifer wells. North/Central FL especially affected.
💨 Rotten egg smell (hydrogen sulfide)H2S gas from anaerobic bacteria in aquifer. Very common in FL — especially Sarasota, Charlotte, Lee, and Manatee counties. Offensive but often not harmful at low levels.
🪨 Mineral scaling / white deposits on fixturesHard water from dissolved calcium and magnesium. FL Floridan aquifer: 200–400+ mg/L hardness is typical — very hard. South FL surficial: moderately hard.
🍵 Tea-colored / yellowish waterTannins — organic compounds from decaying plant material in shallow FL wells. Very common in North FL (Alachua, Columbia, Bradford counties). Not harmful, but stains laundry and tastes earthy.
🦠 Recent flooding near well / after hurricaneColiform bacteria contamination risk after FL flooding. Hurricane flooding can breach well casings. Immediate water testing required — do not drink untested well water after flooding.
🌊 Salty or brackish tasteSaltwater intrusion — critical issue in coastal FL (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Lee, Collier). As FL sea levels rise, saltwater intrusion into shallow aquifers is increasing. Reverse osmosis or water source change may be required.
🌫️ Cloudy / turbid waterSand, silt, or sediment in FL well — often indicates failing well screen or collapsed borehole. FL's sandy soil is particularly prone to screen clogging. May require well rehabilitation or screen replacement.
🧪 Concerned about arsenic / contaminantsFL has some arsenic-elevated areas, especially along paleo-river systems. Also relevant: PFAS contamination near military bases (Pensacola, Eglin AFB area). Test water if in high-risk area.
🔵 Blue/green staining on copper pipes or fixturesLow pH / acidic water corroding copper. Common in shallow FL wells. Pinhole leaks in copper pipes accelerate. Whole-house acid neutralizer (calcite filter) is the standard treatment.

💡 FL Post-Hurricane Well Safety

After any FL hurricane that caused flooding, do not drink or use well water until it has been tested. Flood waters can contaminate the well casing with bacteria, chemicals, and sewage. Our licensed technicians provide post-storm well inspections, shock chlorination, and water testing. Most FL counties waive permit fees for post-hurricane repairs.
