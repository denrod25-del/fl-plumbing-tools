🟤 Copper Pipe
Type L — FL Preferred
Cost installed: $9–14/LF (Type L)
FL Lifespan: 50+ years — longest track record in FL
FL Code: ✅ FPC 2023 §604.1 — Type K, L, M all approved
Insurance: Strongly preferred by Citizens, State Farm, Allstate, Universal. Many FL carriers offer lower premiums for copper-plumbed homes.
FL Concern: Pinhole leaks occur in aggressive soil or acidic water — common in parts of Miami-Dade, Broward, and Charlotte counties. Type L (thicker wall) recommended over Type M in South FL. Request water chemistry report before specifying copper.
✅ 50+ year proven lifespan
✅ Insurance preferred
✅ Best resale value boost
✅ No UV restrictions
✅ FL inspectors know it well
❌ Highest upfront cost
❌ Pinhole leak risk (acidic water)
❌ Rigid — harder to route
❌ Can corrode in sandy soil
❌ Price tied to copper market
🔴 PEX-A Pipe (Uponor / Rehau)
Premium Flexible
Cost installed: $5–9/LF
FL Lifespan: 25–50 years projected (FL-approved since 2003)
FL Code: ✅ FPC §604.3 — ASTM F876/F877, NSF 61 certification required
Insurance: Accepted by Citizens and most FL carriers. Growing acceptance since 2010.
Key Feature: Expansion fittings — no crimp rings. Most flexible PEX type: can be heated with a heat gun to fix kinks. Best freeze resistance of all FL-approved materials (North FL benefit). Must be sleeved or protected from UV if run outdoors.
FL Code Note: 18 inches of copper pipe required adjacent to each water heater connection (FPC §604.3) — applies to all PEX types.
✅ Best freeze resistance
✅ Most flexible routing
✅ No corrosion risk
✅ Self-heals kinks
✅ Cost-effective vs. copper
❌ Not for UV exposure
❌ Chlorine may degrade over decades
❌ Some lenders still flag PEX
❌ Requires copper at WH stub
❌ Projected (not 50-yr proven)
🔵 PEX-B Pipe (SharkBite / Viega / Watts)
Standard Flexible
Cost installed: $4–8/LF
FL Lifespan: 25–50 years projected
FL Code: ✅ FPC §604.3 — ASTM F876, NSF 61 required
Insurance: Widely accepted in FL — most common PEX used in FL repipes since 2008.
Key Feature: Crimp or clamp fittings (simpler, widely available tooling). Stiffer than PEX-A but still flexible enough for most FL applications. Cannot self-heal kinks. Most widely used PEX type in FL. SharkBite push-to-connect fittings popular for repairs (not recommended for in-wall long-term by most FL inspectors).
✅ Lowest price of all options
✅ Most FL plumbers know it
✅ Widely available locally
✅ No corrosion risk
✅ Insurance accepted
❌ Stiffer than PEX-A
❌ Cannot fix kinks
❌ Not for outdoor UV
❌ Projected lifespan only
❌ Crimp tools required
⚪ CPVC Pipe (FlowGuard Gold)
Use with Caution
Cost installed: $5–9/LF
FL Lifespan: 25–40 years — FL heat and humidity accelerate brittleness
FL Code: ✅ FPC §604.2 — ASTM D2846, NSF 61 — but aging concerns apply
Insurance: ⚠️ Citizens and several FL carriers now flag CPVC systems 15+ years old. Some carriers require inspection or add surcharges. Verify with your agent before repiping with CPVC.
FL Concern: CPVC can become brittle in FL's heat, UV exposure, and chemical interactions (certain cleaning products cause stress cracking). Class Action Settlement (2007, Lubrizol/BF Goodrich) involved certain CPVC formulations. Falling out of favor for whole-house repipes in FL. Only use current FlowGuard Gold formulation.
✅ Hot-water rated (180°F)
✅ High pressure rating
✅ No chlorine degradation
✅ Rigid like copper
❌ Brittle over time in FL heat
❌ Growing insurance issues
❌ Recall history (some lots)
❌ Incompatible with PVC cement
❌ Falling out of FL market

⚠️FL Inspector Notes by Material
Copper — Common Failures
📋 Type M used instead of Type L (some inspectors reject in South FL)
📋 Dielectric unions missing at dissimilar metal connections (e.g., copper-to-galvanized)
📋 Support straps missing — code requires every 6 ft horizontal / 10 ft vertical
📋 Flux residue not flushed — causes pinhole acceleration
PEX — Common Failures
📋 18" copper stub at water heater missing (FPC §604.3 — very common failure)
📋 Support missing — code requires every 32 inches horizontal / 4 ft vertical
📋 Barb fittings used inside finished wall cavities (prohibited)
📋 UV exposure without protective sleeve on outdoor runs
📋 NSF 61 certification mark not visible on pipe (inspector may fail)
CPVC — Common Failures
📋 PVC solvent cement used instead of CPVC cement (automatic fail)
📋 Support missing — code requires every 3 ft horizontal / 5 ft vertical
📋 Expansion loops missing on runs over 15 ft (thermal expansion cracking)
📋 Incompatible chemical contact — certain pipe insulations degrade CPVC
🏠FL Homeowner Insurance Guidance
Citizens Property Insurance: Copper preferred; PEX accepted; CPVC systems 15+ years old require inspection — may be rated as "inferior plumbing" affecting eligibility
State Farm: Copper strongly preferred; PEX widely accepted; CPVC on case-by-case basis depending on home age and pipe age
Allstate (FL): All three accepted; possible surcharge on CPVC homes built before 2000; PEX increasingly preferred
Universal Property & Casualty: Copper most preferred; PEX accepted since 2012; CPVC triggers additional underwriting review
Warning: Several FL surplus lines carriers have added CPVC exclusions in 2022–2025 policies. Always verify your carrier's current CPVC policy before repiping.
Pro move: Get a letter from your plumber documenting the new pipe material, brand, and installation date. Some insurers offer 5–15% premium reduction for copper or new PEX repipe.
