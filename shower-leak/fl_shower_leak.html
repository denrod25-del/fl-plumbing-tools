🚨 The FL Shower Dye Test
Pour 1 gallon of food-coloring dye directly into your shower drain (NOT drain cleaner — food coloring only). Wait 15–20 minutes without running any water. Then check: the ceiling below the bathroom, the floor directly adjacent to the shower, and the exterior base of the shower wall. If colored water appears anywhere outside the drain — your shower pan has failed. Stop using the shower immediately and call a licensed plumber.

5 Florida-Specific Shower Leak Facts
1
FL's average home humidity (60–80% RH year-round) accelerates grout failure more than any other single factor. FL grout typically lasts just 5–8 years versus 10–15 years in dry climates like Arizona or Colorado. Annual penetrating grout sealer application is the single most important FL shower maintenance task — it costs $30–80 in materials and can add years of service life.
2
FL's hard water (200+ ppm calcium carbonate) deposits minerals inside grout pores with every shower. As this calcium crust dries and contracts, it cracks the grout matrix from within — a process called efflorescence. This is the #1 cause of FL shower grout failure. A whole-home water softener set to <50 ppm dramatically slows this process and extends all FL plumbing fixtures.
3
Over 70% of FL shower pan leaks originate at the curb (threshold) or drain flange — not across the broad pan surface. The drain flange must be flush with the finished tile surface; raised or sunken flanges create a water pathway around the drain gasket. The curb joint is the most mechanically stressed point in any shower. Both are more repairable than a full pan failure, but still require partial tile removal and professional assessment.
4
In FL's slab-on-grade construction (the dominant building method statewide), a leaking shower pan drains not to a crawlspace or basement — but directly into the void beneath the concrete slab. This moisture promotes mold colonies inside the slab void (which is impossible to remediate without major demolition), corrodes copper supply pipes embedded in the slab, and causes the slab itself to crack or settle over time. Early detection is therefore especially critical in FL.
5
FL Building Code (FBC Chapter 29 / FPC 417) requires all shower pans to pass a flood test — the pan is filled with water to the top of the threshold and held for 24 hours without drainage — before tile installation. Many FL renovation contractors skip this test to save time. If your shower was remodeled without a permit and no flood test was performed, you may have had a leaking pan from day one without knowing it.

🏠 FL Homeowners Insurance — Shower Leaks
Many FL homeowners policies cover "sudden and accidental" water damage from shower pan failure. This coverage can include ceiling replacement, drywall, flooring replacement, and mold remediation in the subfloor — but only if reported promptly and only if you have not done repairs first. Always photograph all damage extensively, call your insurer before any work begins, and obtain a written licensed-plumber diagnosis letter for the adjuster. Slow leaks (gradual damage over months) are typically NOT covered — only sudden failures are.
🔧 FL Annual Shower Maintenance Schedule
Every 12–18 months: Apply penetrating grout sealer to all grout lines.
Every 3–4 years: Remove and replace all corner and base caulk with 100% silicone.
Every 5 years: Perform the food-coloring dye test to confirm pan integrity.

This $50–200/year maintenance routine prevents $5,000–25,000 in shower pan replacement and water damage repairs. FL's humidity makes this schedule non-negotiable.

⚠️ If You Found a Problem
Hollow tiles or water below bathroom: Stop using shower immediately. Call a plumber today — not next week.

Mold behind tile: Do not bleach or paint over it. Mold behind FL shower tile grows rapidly in our humidity and may have penetrated the subfloor. Professional assessment required before any repair.

Cracked grout or failed caulk: Address within 90 days. In FL, even small grout gaps allow moisture to penetrate and grow into major pan damage within 6–18 months.
