📋 FPC DWV Code — Key Requirements
FPC Chapter 7 — Sanitary Drainage

FPC 701.1 — Scope: DWV system shall convey sewage and liquid wastes to public sewer or approved private disposal system. FL requires FL-licensed plumbing contractor for all DWV work.

FPC 704.1 — Slope: Horizontal drainage piping shall be run in practical alignment and a uniform slope of not less than ¼ unit vertical per foot (2%) for pipes 3" and smaller. Pipes 4" and larger may be sloped at ⅛" per foot minimum (1.04%).

FPC 704.2 — Minimum size: No horizontal waste or soil pipe shall be smaller than the largest trap discharge it serves. Minimum 3" for water closets and urinals.

FPC 704.3 — Change of direction: Horizontal-to-vertical changes shall use 45° wyes or combo fittings. No 90° sanitary tees in horizontal-to-horizontal runs. No double sanitary tees.

FPC 707.1 — Prohibited fittings: Prohibited in DWV: running traps, double-hub fittings used backwards, bell-and-spigot on PVC without approved coupling, copper fittings with lead joints.

FPC Chapter 9 — Vents

FPC 901.1 — Venting required: Every trap and trapped fixture shall be vented in accordance with this chapter.

FPC 904.1 — Air admittance valves (AAV): Permitted in FL under FPC 918 for individual fixtures and branch vents. AAVs prohibited as the only vent on a drainage stack. Must be accessible and installed per ASSE 1051 (island fixtures) or ASSE 1049.
