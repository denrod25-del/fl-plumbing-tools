⚠️When Is a Plumbing Permit Required in FL?
Always Required (FL Building Code §105)
✅ New water heater installation
✅ Whole-house or partial repipe
✅ Sewer lateral replacement
✅ Gas line installation, repair, or extension
✅ New bathroom / plumbing fixture addition
✅ Backflow preventer installation (commercial & multi-family)
✅ Water service replacement (meter to house)
✅ Pool heater installation
✅ Sprinkler system installation
Generally NOT Required
🔧 Faucet / valve replacement (same location)
🔧 Toilet replacement (like-for-like)
🔧 Showerhead replacement
🔧 Garbage disposal replacement
🔧 Minor drain clearing (rooter service)
🔧 Water heater element replacement (electric)
⚠️ FL unpermitted work: Unpermitted plumbing work is a serious issue in Florida. Insurance claims can be denied if unpermitted work is involved. Home sales can fall through during inspection. Citizens Insurance now requires permit history for water heater installations. Always use a licensed CFC contractor who pulls the permit.

⏱️Common Permit Delays in Florida
Top Reasons for Permit Delays
❌ Incomplete applications: Missing scope of work, square footage, fixture count, or valuation
❌ Missing contractor license #: FL requires CFC license number and qualifier signature on all plumbing permits
❌ HOA approval pending: Many FL county portals flag HOA properties — approval letter required before permit can issue
❌ Backlog at building dept: Miami-Dade, Broward, and Hillsborough frequently run 2–3x normal queue times after hurricanes
❌ Failed first inspection: Re-inspection adds 5–10 business days to final close-out
❌ Open permit from prior owner: Older unpermitted or unfinished work must be finalized before new permits issue
Pro tip: Most FL counties now offer online permit portals. Miami-Dade uses iBuild, Broward uses MyPermitNow, Palm Beach uses MyGovHub. Licensed contractors can expedite review in most counties by applying with a complete, digital package. Ask your plumber if they use a permit expediting service.

🔍Inspection Guide by Phase
1. Rough-In Inspection
What inspector checks:
✅ Pipe sizing per FPC §604 (supply) & §704 (DWV)
✅ All connections accessible — walls open, no drywall
✅ Water supply pipe material (copper, PEX, CPVC)
✅ DWV slope: 1/4" per foot for horizontal drains
✅ Cleanout access per FPC §708
✅ Venting — minimum 2" vent stack, proper tie-ins
✅ Pipe supports per FPC §305 (every 6 ft horizontal)
✅ Anti-scald protection devices in showers (ASSE 1016)
Common failures: Missing cleanout access, DWV slope under 1/4", vent stack too small, PEX without approved manifold system in strict FL counties.
2. Pressure Test Inspection
What inspector checks:
✅ Water supply pressure test: 100 PSI for 15 minutes
✅ No pressure drop during hold period
✅ All caps, plugs, and temporary connections visible
✅ Gas line test: 15 PSI air test for 15 minutes (where applicable)
✅ Inspector may require fill with water, not air, for supply test
Common failures: Pinhole leak from fitting not fully seated, compression fitting failure, slab penetrations leaking from thermal expansion during test.
3. DWV (Drain-Waste-Vent) Inspection
What inspector checks:
✅ All drain lines properly sloped (1/4" per foot minimum)
✅ Trap size matches fixture unit count
✅ P-traps — no S-traps allowed in FL
✅ Vent pipes rise to roof — minimum 6" above roof deck
✅ Wet venting compliance (FPC §910)
✅ No hub connections or bare ABS (ABS not allowed in FL)
✅ PVC Schedule 40 or DWV grade throughout
Common failures: S-traps (not allowed under FPC), insufficient venting (especially in slab-on-grade FL homes), drain lines under slab without prior documentation.
4. Final / Certificate of Completion Inspection
What inspector checks:
✅ All fixtures installed and operational
✅ Water heater connections complete — supply, discharge, pan, TPR
✅ All walls closed and finished
✅ Meter set by utility — water service active
✅ Pressure reducing valve (PRV) installed if needed (street >80 PSI)
✅ All permits and inspections complete and signed off
✅ Plumber's final signature on permit card or digital sign-off
✅ After final: Certificate of Completion (CC) issued. Job is done. Keep your CC on file — needed for home sales, insurance renewals, and future permit applications.
