🆚 Heat Pump vs Gas — FL Comparison

🔥 Gas Heater (NG or LP)

Fastest Heat Higher Operating Cost Works Below 55°F

Heats pool quickly (2–4 hrs). Works in any FL temperature, including cold snaps. Efficiency: 82–95% (condensing). Best for infrequent heating, spas, or quick warmup. NG: ~$0.80–1.20/therm. LP: ~$2.50–3.50/gallon. Typical monthly FL cost: $80–$250 (NG) or $200–$600 (LP).

⚡ Heat Pump

Lowest Operating Cost COP 4–6x Slower Heatup

FL's warm climate is ideal for heat pumps. COP of 4–6 means $1 of electricity = $4–6 of heat. Best for maintaining temperature daily. Works well above 55°F — FL rarely dips below that. Typical monthly FL cost: $30–$100. Slower: 8–24 hrs for full heatup from cold.

🔄 Hybrid System

Best of Both FL Recommended

Heat pump as primary (low-cost operation), gas as backup for cold FL nights or rapid heatup for parties. Most cost-effective long-term for FL homeowners who use pool year-round. Payback vs gas-only: 3–5 years.

Gas pipe is black iron or CSST (not galvanized or copper for NG)
CSST bonded with #6 AWG copper within 6 ft of meter
Sediment trap / drip leg installed at heater connection
Manual shutoff valve within 6 ft of heater (accessible)
Heater has 18" clearance from any combustible
Pressure test performed (10 PSI air for 15 min — FL code)
Heater is listed/labeled (AGA, CSA, or UL)
FL pool plumbing permit applied for (separate from gas permit)

📋 FL Pool Heater & Gas Code Requirements

Florida pools require two separate permits: a pool/equipment permit (FL Building Code Chapter 34) AND a gas permit (FPC Section 406) when a gas heater is installed. Both require a licensed contractor.
FL Pool Equipment Permit (FBC Chapter 34) — Any pool heater installation or replacement requires a permit. Licensed pool contractor (CPO) or licensed plumbing contractor (CPC) may pull permit. Plans must show heater location, gas stub-out, and equipment pad dimensions.
Gas Permit — FL Plumbing Code Section 406 — Separate gas permit required for any new gas line or heater connection. Licensed plumbing contractor (CPC) with gas certification required in Florida. Gas line must pass pressure test (10 PSI air, 15 minutes) before inspection.
NFPA 54 — Gas Pipe Sizing Required — All FL residential gas installations must comply with NFPA 54 (National Fuel Gas Code). Pipe size must accommodate total BTU demand including all connected appliances, not just the pool heater. Undersized pipe = failed inspection.
CSST Bonding (FL Code Section 406.6) — Corrugated stainless steel tubing (CSST) must be electrically bonded to grounding electrode system with #6 AWG copper within 6 feet of meter. Required since 2009 FL code adoption. Pool heater bonding to pool water bonding grid: separate requirement (NFPA 70).
Pool Electrical Bonding (NFPA 70 / FBC) — All metallic pool equipment including gas heater shell must be bonded to #8 AWG copper bonding grid. Pool water bonding via sacrificial electrode. Inspector will check heater bonding lug connection. Gas heater + pool = two separate bonding requirements.
Setback / Clearance Requirements — Gas heater: 18" from combustibles, 36" service clearance. FL pool barrier: heater must be outside pool barrier or barrier must be continuous. Enclosure (screen room): heater must have combustion air per manufacturer — confirm ventilation adequate. Coastal FL: corrosion-resistant materials required within 1 mile of salt water.
Propane (LP) in FL — Additional Rules — LP tank permit required (NFPA 58). Above-ground LP tank: minimum 10 ft from pool equipment. Underground LP tank: minimum 10 ft from pool. LP regulator and second-stage regulator required. LP gas heater must be rated for LP (not NG) — not interchangeable without conversion kit.
FL Contractor License Requirements — Gas line for pool heater: Licensed CPC (Certified Plumbing Contractor) with Section 1 gas license OR licensed pool contractor with gas endorsement. Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach: local license often required in addition. Do NOT allow unlicensed "pool guys" to run gas lines — creates serious liability and void insurance.

Florida Quick Answers

How much does pool heater gas cost in Florida?

On this page, Florida pool heater gas estimates run about $4,500-$8,000, depending on home size, materials, and project scope. Use the calculator above for a Florida-specific estimate.

What affects the price?

Pricing depends on the size and layout of your home, the pipe materials and fixtures you choose, your Florida region and local labor rates, and permit fees. Work that is more complex or harder to access generally costs more.

Can I DIY this, or should I hire a licensed plumber?

In Florida, minor maintenance may be DIY, but anything beyond that generally calls for a licensed plumber, and many jobs require a permit and inspection. When a permit, gas work, or your main water or drain lines are involved, hire a Florida-licensed plumber.

Does homeowners insurance cover it?

It depends on the cause and your specific policy. Sudden, accidental damage is more often covered than gradual wear-and-tear or maintenance - confirm the details with your insurer.

How long does it take?

Timelines depend on scope - many routine jobs take a few hours to a day, while larger projects run longer. Your licensed plumber can confirm after assessing your home.
