⛏️ Spot Repair — Best When:

• First known slab leak in a newer home (under 20 yrs copper)
• Single, precisely located leak point
• Budget-conscious repair (insurance paying for repair, not remediation)
• Tile pattern still available or owner accepts repair patch
• Pipe system otherwise in good condition

FL plumber reality: Most FL plumbers recommend against spot repair for homes with 30+ year copper under slab — another leak will likely appear within 1–3 years. Consider rerouting or repipe if home is pre-1990.

🏛️ FL Homeowner Insurance & Leak Claims
What FL HO Policies Typically Cover

Sudden and accidental discharge: FL homeowner policies generally cover sudden, accidental water damage from a plumbing failure — for example, a pipe that suddenly bursts and floods a room. This typically covers water damage remediation and drying.

What's usually excluded: Most FL policies exclude the cost of repairing or replacing the failed pipe itself (only the resulting damage). "Continuous or repeated leakage" is excluded — if an insurer determines you knew about a leak and didn't fix it, they may deny the claim entirely. Flood damage (from outside water) is excluded from standard HO — requires separate NFIP flood policy.

FL-Specific Coverage Issues

Citizens Insurance (FL's insurer of last resort) covers sudden water damage but is aggressively denying slow-leak claims. Citizens has implemented water leak coverage restrictions in recent years — review your specific policy carefully.

Assignment of Benefits (AOB): FL significantly restricted AOB in 2023 (SB 2-A). You now generally cannot sign over your insurance rights to a contractor before your insurer is notified. Work with your adjuster before committing to repairs for insurance claim purposes.

Slab leak coverage: FL policies vary widely. Some cover detection costs (breaking slab to find pipe) but not the pipe repair. "Ordinance or law" coverage in your policy matters when FL building code requires upgrading old pipes to current standards during repair — this upgrade cost may be covered.

FL Insurance Claim Steps — Slab Leak
1
Stop the water — shut off main valve at meter immediately
Document the shutoff time; note any active discharge / flooding; take photos and video before anything is moved or dried
2
Notify your insurance company within 24–48 hours
Do NOT wait — FL policies often require prompt notification; delays can void coverage; call the 24-hr claims number, not just the agent
3
Begin emergency water extraction and drying (with insurer approval)
Most FL insurers will authorize emergency mitigation immediately; use a licensed FL water remediation company; document all drying equipment, moisture readings, and daily reports
4
Get leak detection performed and documented
The detection report (with exact leak location, cause, and method used) is a key claim document; the adjuster will need this; many FL insurers cover leak detection costs
5
Wait for adjuster inspection before permanent repairs
Do NOT pour concrete, re-tile, or close walls before the adjuster sees the damage; document everything open; temporary patches are OK; permanent repairs before adjuster inspection can void coverage
6
Get your own licensed plumber estimate — do not rely solely on insurer's preferred vendor
FL law allows you to use your own licensed contractor; get 2–3 estimates; FL has a "right to choose" — you don't have to use the insurer's contractor for repairs
7
If denied — file for FL Department of Financial Services mediation
FL DFS insurance mediation program (1-877-My-FL-CFO) offers free mediation for denied claims under $500,000; also consider a licensed FL public adjuster for complex slab leak claims
⚖️ FL Law: FL Stat. 627.7011 requires insurers to pay the ACV (actual cash value) of covered damage promptly while any depreciation disputes are resolved. You should receive an initial payment quickly — if not, contact FL DFS. FL also has a 90-day repair completion rule for insurers after agreement.
Mold & Water Damage Escalation (FL)

FL mold risk timeline: FL's heat and humidity create ideal mold conditions. Mold can begin growing within 24–48 hours of water intrusion. After 72 hours, active mold growth is likely in wet drywall and insulation. After 1 week, mold remediation costs can exceed the original plumbing repair by 5–10×.

Mold remediation costs in FL: $15–$30/sq ft for affected area; average FL whole-room mold remediation: $3,000–$8,000; crawl space or attic mold from slow leak: $5,000–$15,000. FL mold coverage in standard HO policies is typically capped at $10,000 or excluded entirely — check your policy.
